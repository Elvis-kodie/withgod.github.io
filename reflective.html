Reflective Practice

Reflective practice is about taking a step back to think honestly about what you did, how you felt, and what you learned from an experience. It’s a way to make sense of your actions and decisions so you can grow, improve, and handle similar situations better in the future.

During my internship at ABUAKWA M/A J.H.S "B", where I taught Computing in B.S.7, I consistently reflected on nearly every lesson I delivered. However, this account specifically focuses on a lesson I taught on February 17th, 2025.

Lesson Coverage

  • Name of School: Abuakwa M/A J.H.S ‘B’
  • Subject: Computing
  • Strand: Communication Networks
  • Sub Strand: Internet and Social Media
  • Class: B.S.7
  • Class Size: 51
  • Date: 17th February, 2025
  • Lesson Duration: 86 minutes

Instructional Objectives:

  1. Learners will be able to define the Internet and Social Media.
  2. Describe at least three (3) uses of social media sites.

Reference: Mantey, E.H. (2023). Computing Basic 7-9. Archivers Publications. (P.153)

Teacher–Learner Activities

Teaching and Learning Materials: Computer, Projector, Textbook, Pictures and videos of social media sites.

Relevant Previous Knowledge (R.P.K): Used questions and answers to review learners’ previous knowledge.

Mode of Delivery: Demonstration and Guided practice.

Introduction

I quickly reviewed students’ prior knowledge using a short test. Then I introduced the new topic and shared the performance indicators.

Development of the Lesson

  • Activity 1: Guided learners on how to connect to the internet and use web browsers (Google Chrome, Mozilla Firefox) to search information safely.
  • Activity 2: Introduced social media platforms like Facebook, WhatsApp, Instagram; discussed their uses, and examined their impact.

Closure

Summarized the lesson. Students asked questions and received an assignment on social media advantages/disadvantages.

Strength

The learning objectives were successfully achieved. Strong subject knowledge helped maintain students' interest. Active participation and engagement during hands-on activities were notable.

Challenges

  • Some students couldn’t differentiate between web browsers and social media platforms.
  • Misconceptions about online safety and privacy.
  • Limited time affected lesson depth.

This showed me I needed to balance explanation with individual understanding checks.

What I Would Do Differently Next Time

  • Encourage more student questions and practice.
  • Plan better to fit time allocation.
  • Use more visual and hands-on materials.

Innovative Measures

I consulted my mentor who guided me with strategies to support learners struggling with Computing. This taught me to be flexible and to reinforce foundational computing skills before tackling complex topics.

Insight Gained

  • Interactive methods keep learners engaged.
  • Strong subject knowledge boosts teacher confidence and student focus.
  • Guided discovery helps students learn better than direct instruction alone.
